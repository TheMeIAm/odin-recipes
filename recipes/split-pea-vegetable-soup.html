Split Pea Vegetable Soup

from The Bais Yaakov Cookbook

The perfect, hearty, filling soup!

This soup will transport you back to your grandmother's kitchen: healthy, hearty, warm and packed with TLC. Lots of veggies to chop, but well worth the effort, especially since it makes a lot!

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2 marrow bones or 1 lb flanken (optional)
  • 1 cup dried green split peas, picked over, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup dried green split peas, picked over, rinsed and drained
  • 3 medium zucchini, diced
  • 4 carrots, diced
  • 1 sweet potato, diced
  • 1 Idaho potato, diced
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 tablespoons salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Heat 2 tablespoons oil in 8-quart pot over medium heat. Add onion, celery, garlic, and marrow bones or flanken if using. Saute about 10 minutes.
  2. Add dried green and yellow split peas to pot. Fill pot about 2/3 with water. Bring to a boil. Cover and reduce heat. Simmer 2 hours.
  3. Add zucchini, carrots, sweet potato, idaho potato, sugar, salt and ground black pepper. Cover pot and cook 1 more hour.
  4. Remove marrow bones, if using, and discard. If using flanken, cut up and serve with soup.
